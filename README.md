# Social Media Analytics for Strategic Branding – PepsiCo

This project is a complete Excel-based social media analytics case study built on the PepsiCo Marketing Dataset.  
The objective was to clean raw data, analyze engagement, platform performance, content strategy, campaign effectiveness, and audience loyalty, and derive data-backed insights.

This project follows the task structure provided in the assignment and strictly uses data-driven analysis.

---

## Project Overview

PepsiCo operates across multiple platforms including Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube.  
The dataset includes post-level data, engagement summaries, and campaign metadata.

The goal of this analysis was to:
- Understand what content performs best
- Identify the strongest platforms
- Evaluate campaign effectiveness
- Analyze follower growth and loyalty
- Support marketing decisions using data

---

## Dataset Source

The dataset was provided via an external link as part of the assignment.  
Due to source restrictions, the raw dataset is not uploaded to this repository.  
All analysis was performed directly using the provided dataset.

---

## Tasks Covered

### Task 1 – Data Preprocessing and Cleaning

The raw dataset contained duplicates, inconsistent naming, mixed date formats, unstructured hashtags, and formatting issues in numeric columns.  
After cleaning and standardization, the data became fully structured and reliable for analysis.

This step ensured:
- consistent platform and content type names  
- properly formatted numeric columns  
- clean hashtag structure for analysis  

---

### Task 2 – Engagement Analysis

Engagement rate was calculated for each post using:
(Likes + Shares + Comments) / Impressions

Key findings:
- YouTube and Facebook lead in average engagement
- Instagram shows the lowest engagement
- Top 10 posts are dominated by Carousels and Reels from Twitter and YouTube
- Carousel and Video content generate the strongest response
- #PepsiCoRefresh is the top-performing hashtag
- #ThirstyForMore and #LiveForNow follow in ranking

---

### Task 3 – Social Media Platform Analysis

Key findings:
- Instagram has the highest engagement rate
- Instagram also shows the strongest net follower growth
- Ad spend vs engagement visualization shows varying efficiency across platforms
- Based on engagement, growth, and consistency, the data supports focusing on:
  **Instagram and Facebook as core platforms**

---

### Task 4 – Hashtag & Content Strategy

Key findings:
- #PepsiCoRefresh is the most frequently used hashtag
- #BetterWithPepsi and #SmoothLikeNitroPepsi show stronger average performance
- Carousels outperform Images and Videos across platforms

Platform-wise content performance:
- Facebook – Carousels and Reels perform better  
- Instagram – Images and Reels perform better  
- Twitter – Text and Carousels perform better  
- YouTube – Text and Story content perform better  

Content strategy should be platform-specific.

---

### Task 5 – Campaign Effectiveness

Key findings:
- Engagement is higher during campaign periods compared to before
- ROI analysis shows **DailyWellness campaign has the highest return on engagement vs budget**
- Follower growth analysis shows **DailyWellness has the strongest follower growth impact**

Not all campaigns perform equally. DailyWellness stands out as the most effective.

---

### Task 6 – Follower Retention & Audience Loyalty

Key findings:
- Follower growth varies across time and platforms
- **October has the highest net follower gain**
- Moving average analysis shows a positive overall growth trend
- Ad spend and follower growth show a positive correlation, but not perfectly linear

This indicates that both budget and content strategy influence audience growth.

## Key Learnings

- Data cleaning is critical before any analysis
- Visual content formats drive stronger engagement
- Instagram is the strongest platform for both engagement and growth
- Carousels and Reels perform better across platforms
- DailyWellness is the most effective campaign in terms of ROI and follower impact
- Audience growth is influenced by both ad spend and content quality
